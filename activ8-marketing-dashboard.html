Background on Short Selling

Investment strategy fundamentals

Short selling is an investment strategy where an investor borrows shares of a stock and immediately sells them, with the intention of buying them back later at a lower price. The investor profits if the stock price declines and loses money if it rises.

How It Works

  1. Borrow shares from a broker
  2. Sell shares at current market price
  3. Wait for price to decline (hopefully)
  4. Buy back shares at lower price
  5. Return shares to broker
  6. Keep the difference as profit

Purpose in Markets

  • Price Discovery: Helps correct overvalued stocks
  • Market Efficiency: Provides liquidity and opposing viewpoints
  • Risk Management: Allows hedging strategies
  • Fraud Detection: Often uncovers corporate malfeasance

Activist Short Selling

Beyond traditional shorting — forensic research meets public advocacy

Activist short selling goes far beyond traditional short selling. Activist short sellers conduct extensive forensic research, publish detailed reports alleging fraud, accounting irregularities, or fundamental business problems, and actively promote their thesis through media, social media, and industry channels.

Research-Intensive

Activist shorts produce comprehensive reports (often 50–100+ pages) with:

  • Forensic accounting analysis
  • On-the-ground investigation
  • Supply chain verification
  • Expert interviews
  • Document analysis
  • Whistleblower information

Public Campaigns

Unlike traditional shorts who quietly take positions, activist shorts:

  • Publish detailed research reports publicly
  • Engage with media (Bloomberg, CNBC, WSJ, etc.)
  • Use Twitter/FinTwit extensively to share findings
  • Present at conferences
  • May engage with regulators

Market Impact

When prominent activist short sellers publish research:

  • Stock prices can drop 20–50%+ immediately
  • Trading volume spikes dramatically
  • Media coverage intensifies
  • Companies often issue responses/rebuttals
  • Regulatory investigations may follow
